1. Philosophy/All (Harvard Fall Tournament 2010)
This author's essay “Violence and Metaphysics” considered the works of Emmanuel Levinas, and this author addressed Austin's speech-act theory in “Signature Event Context,” which drew heavy criticism from John Searle. He termed the point at which the realization of foundational contradictions arises an “aporia,” from the Greek for ‘puzzle’ or ‘confusion,’ and posited this point as unavoidable in rigorous analysis of any text. For 10 points, name this French-Algerian philosopher, author of On Grammatology and developer of deconstruction.
ANSWER: Jacques Derrida

2. Philosophy/All (Minnesota Novice Set 2011)

ANSWER: Confucianism [accept word forms like Confucius or Confucian; accept K’ung-fu-tzu or Master K’ung]

8. Philosophy/All (Delta Burke 2003)
The ancestry of this scientific theory has been traced to Plato’s Timaeus, Teilhard de Chardin’s theory of the noosphere, and Hutton’s idea of a “superorganism.” An analogy with giant redwoods which are nearly 99% dead matter make it more believable and evidence showing that marine phytoplankton emitting cloud-condensation nuclei might influence weather also supports it. However the best evidence for it is still the stability of earth’s atmospheric gases. It was first proposed in 1979 book subtitled A New Look at Life on Earth written by James Lovelock. FTP name this theory which holds that that the earth is a survival-conscious, self-regulating, living entity.
ANSWER: Gaia Hypothesis.
