1. Literature/All (HSAPQ Tournament 11 2009)
This poet lamented the loss of "that store...counted best" that now "in ashes lie" in one poem. Another poem by this writer claims that "friends, less wise than true" "snatcht" "the ill-formed offspring of [this poet's] feeble brain." Those works by this poet are titled "Upon the Burning of Our House" and "The Author to Her Book." Some of this woman's works were published without her knowledge by her brother-in-law in England. For 10 points, name this seventeenth century American female poet of The Tenth Muse Lately Sprung Up in America.
ANSWER: Anne Bradstreet
