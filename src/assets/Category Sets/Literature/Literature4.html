1. Literature/All (Weekend of Quizbowl Saturday Event 2009)
Based on a John Luther Long short story, Act II of this work ends with a group of characters singing the wordless “Coro a bocca chiusa”, or “Humming Chorus”. Its main male character, a spinto tenor, details his “intoxication” in the aria “Amore o grillo” before laying out his wandering philosophy in “Dovunque al mondo”. Its title character reads the inscription on her father’s knife and sings “Con onor muore” before killing herself, though she’d earlier dreamed of the male lead’s return in “Un bel di, vedremo”. For 10 points, identify this opera in which Prince Yamadori is refused by Cio Cio San, who herself is stood up by Pinkerton, a work of Giacomo Puccini.
ANSWER: Madame Butterfly [or Madama Butterfly]
