1. Literature/All (LIST 2013)
A work often paired with this one mentions the title figure’s “clothing of delight” and “tender voice” after asking “does thou know who made thee?” This poem’s narrator wonders if “stars threw down their spears / and watered heaven with their tears” before asking “did he who made the lamb make thee?” This poem asks “what the hammer? what the chain?” produced the title figure, whose “fearful symmetry” was surely framed by some “immortal eye.” For ten points, identify this William Blake poem whose title animal is “burning bright / in the forests of the night.”
ANSWER: “The Tyger”
