1. Literature/All (HFT 2009)
One poem in this collection states, “The poet resembles this prince of cloud and sky who frequent the tempest and laughs at the bowman.” Another poem in this work reads, “you who, like the stab of a knife, entered my plaintive heart.” This collection includes the poems “The Albatross,” and “The Vampire.” This poetry collection contains sections titled “Revolt,” “Wine,” and “Death,” and its first section is called “Spleen and Ideal.” For 10 points, identify this poetry collection by Charles Baudelaire.
ANSWER: The Flowers of Evil [or Les Fleurs du Mal]

2. Literature/All (PACE NSC 2004)
The book opens with the narrator explaining why he lives in the basement and takes the current to operate 1369 lights from Monopoly Light and Power. The narrator then returns to his adolescence and tells of being forced to fight for the amusement of whites and driving a philanthropist around while in college. For 10 points, name this book about a man who goes unnoticed much of the time, written by Ralph Ellison.
ANSWER: Invisible Man

3. Literature/All (BHSAT 2014)
