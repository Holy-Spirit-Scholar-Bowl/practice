1. Literature/All (HFT 2008)
This novel's protagonist meets Father Vincent while staying at Mrs. Lithebe's Mission, and that protagonist's siblings include a carpenter-turned-politician and a younger sister who turned to prostitution. In addition to John and Gertrude, Theophilus Msimangu helps this novel's protagonist. 2. Literature/All (Weekend of Quizbowl Saturday Event 2009)
He closes his poem “From the Frontier of Writing” by leaving “posted soldiers flowing and receding like tree shadows into the polished windscreen” and finds the title berry to “take the roaming shape of Diogenes” in his collection The Haw Lantern, while he wrote about his father aging, contrasted with his earlier abilities with a horse-plough in “Follower.” For ten points, identify this poet who wrote about his four-year-old brother's death while he was at school in “Mid-Term Break” and promises that he will follow his father and grandfather by “Digging” the earth with his “squat pen,” best known for his recent translation of Beowulf.
ANSWER: Seamus Heaney

3. Literature/All (QuAC I 2008)
This city is where Frankie Machine struggles to stay off heroin after leaving prison in Nelson Algren's The Man with the Golden Arm. It is also the city in which Mary Dalton is suffocated by Bigger Thomas in Richard Wright's Native Son, though it is more famously described in one work as the “Hog butcher of the world”. For 10 points, name this city described as having “big shoulders” by Carl Sandburg.
