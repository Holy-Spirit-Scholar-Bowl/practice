1. Literature/All (Chitin 2008)
Sherwood Anderson wrote a noted tribute to this author in Horses and Men. He wrote about tycoon Frank Cowperwood in The Financier, The Titan and The Stoic, his so-called Trilogy of Desire. One of his novels focuses on the title character leaving the country for Chicago, where she begins an affair with George Hurstwood, while his first success came in a book that tells of social climber Clyde Griffiths drowning the pregnant Roberta Alden to avoid marrying her. For 10 points, name this author of Sister Carrie and An American Tragedy.
ANSWER: Theodore Dreiser

2. Literature/All (SCOP Novice 2010)
In the Talmud, a man with this name is the son of Pandeira, and is condemned to death by the Sanhedrin. He is classified in Baha'i as a Manifestation of God, and in Islam, he spoke out as a baby, saying that he would reveal the Injeel. He was raised up to heaven alive, though he will eventually descend to slay the Dajjal. In Christianity, he was killed by the Romans at Calvary. Islam and Christianity agree that he was conceived immaculately by the Virgin Mary. Identify this Abrahamic religious figure, worshiped primarily by Christians as the Messiah.
ANSWER: Jesus Christ [accept either or both parts; accept Isa; accept Yeshua; prompt on Messiah before the end]

13. Literature/All (Illinois Earlybird 2006)
Called the “self-created, to whom none had given birth,” the “lord of divine words,” and the “heart of Ra,” this patron of the arts, hieroglyphics, science, and wisdom was believed to be the author of the Book of the Dead. When Horus and Set fought, he was the judge, and one of his most important duties was to write down the results of the weighing of the heart of a deceased entering the afterlife. FTP, name this ibis- headed Egyptian god often associated with writing.
ANSWER: Thoth
