6. Geography/All (Brookwood Invitational Scholars Bowl 2013)
One type of these trees are called “Jarrahs.” A species of these trees near Koolpin Gorge is found in Kakadu National Park. The secretion of tiny droplets of oil by these trees led to the naming of the Blue Mountains. The “scribbly” type of this tree secretes a namesake sticky sap. The leaves of these trees provide very few nutrients, so their most notable inhabitant sleeps 20 hours a day.
