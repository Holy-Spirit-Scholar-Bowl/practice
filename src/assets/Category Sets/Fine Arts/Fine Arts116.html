1. Fine Arts/All (HFT 2009)
This work's fourth movement begins with timpani sextuplets. The English horn and oboe play a “ranz des vaches” duet representing a dialogue between two shepherds in the third movement, which is titled “Scene in the Fields.” Its final movement has a famous E-flat clarinet solo and features a bastardized version of the idee fixe theme. 4. Fine Arts/All (QuAC I 2008)
This work opens with the title character mocking Count Ceprano because the duke is seducing Ceprano's wife at that very moment. 9. Fine Arts/All (RAVE 2012)
At measure 109 in the first movement of this work, a sustained B pedal is played by the first horn and second bassoon. The second movement of this work, which was given to Anselm Hottenbrenner and discovered by his brother, ends with an E major chord and also features a clarinet solo over strings; that section is named Andante con moto. Some historians theorize that the entr’acte to the composer’s incidental music to Rosamunde was meant to serve as a sketch for the ending of this work.
