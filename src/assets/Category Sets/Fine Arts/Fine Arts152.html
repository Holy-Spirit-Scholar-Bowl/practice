1. Fine Arts/All (MOHIT (Thomas Jefferson) 2009)
Gilbert and Sullivan parodied a scene from act three of this work in Ruddigore, and Giovanni Bottesini wrote a fantasy based on themes from it. Like many of the composers' works, it is a bel canto opera. The opera begins with a search for an intruder at a castle, and Normanno reveals that the titular character has been secretly meeting Edgardo because he saved her from a raging bull. The opera concludes with Edgardo stabbing himself so that he can be reunited with the title character in heaven. In a notable aria, Il dolce suono, the titular character goes mad and kills her new husband Arturo. FTP, name this opera, based on a novel by Sir Walter Scott, written by Gaetano Donizetti.
ANSWER: Lucia di Lamermoor

2. Fine Arts/All (Prison Bowl 2009)
Many of this man's works were produced by family friends Hans and Florence Knoll, including one with a white frame and red cushion, the iconic Tulip chair. Collaborating with the president of the Cranbrook Academy of Art, he designed the Kleinhans Music Hall in Buffalo, New York. The roof of another of his buildings has 1/8th of a sphere, and serves as a concert hall in MIT. His other works include the General Motors Technical Center, the TWA flight center in New York, and Dulles International Airport Terminal near Washington, D.C. For 10 points, name this architect who designed the Gateway Arch in St. Louis, Missouri, a man of Finnish descent.
ANSWER: Eero Saarinen

3. Fine Arts/All (Prison Bowl 2013)
This man wrote the aria La Deplorable Fete, where the opera’s titular women prepare to commit mass suicide when Greek soldiers demand treasure. He also wrote the orchestral arrangement to Weber’s Invitation to the Dance. A viola represents the title character of his second symphony as he finds company with a dangerous crowd in the fourth movement titled “Orgie de brigards”. In addition to writing Les Troyens and Harold in Italy, he created a work where an artist is fixated on a woman associated with a clarinet motif also called the “idée fixe”. After taking opium, the artist dreams an execution in “March to the Scaffold” that sends him to a “Witches’ Sabbath” in hell. For 10 points, name this composer of Symphonie Fantastique.
ANSWER: Hector Berlioz

4. Fine Arts/All (Prison Bowl 2014)
In Republic X, Socrates describes an ancient quarrel between philosophy and this field, a counterfeit imitation that spreads false role models. Socrates then concludes that it must be banished from the Republic. The Apollonian and Dionysian aspects of this field are detailed in a work by Nietzsche that describes its birth from the spirit of music. A work on this field describes its use of hamartia, or tragic flaws. Aristotle constructed a hierarchy of its six parts, where mythos and ethos are valued over opsis, or spectacle. That work, Poetics, also posits that this form of art purges emotions through catharsis. For 10 points, name this art form performed by actors.
ANSWER: drama [or tragedy; accept poetry until “Poetics”; prompt on theater]

5. Fine Arts/All (HSAPQ Colonia 2 2011)
On this instrument, one can play notes separated by a twelfth by hitting the register key. This instrument is typically assembled from five parts, including the barrel and the bell. Aaron Copland wrote a concerto for this instrument for Benny Goodman, and this instrument plays the opening solo glissando in George Gershwin’s Rhapsody in Blue. For 20 points, name this woodwind staple of concert bands, a single reed instrument with a pitch lower than the flute.
ANSWER: (B-flat) clarinet

15. Fine Arts/All (WUHSAC XI 2009)
This composer’s first two operas, Die Feen and Das Liebesverbot, were poorly received, leaving him in considerable financial difficulty. Fleeing his debtors, he took a stormy sea passage to London, which inspired his opera about a captain doomed to sail until Judgment Day. In 1849, he introduced the concept of Gesamtkunstwerk (guh-zahmt-koonst-verk), which featured prominently in his next opera, Lohengrin. With funding from King Ludwig II of Bavaria, he built the Bayreuth (bahy-roit) Festspielhaus, which opened for the premiere of his four-opera cycle. For ten points, name this German composer of Der Ring des Nibelungen.
ANSWER: Richard Wagner (VAHG-ner)
