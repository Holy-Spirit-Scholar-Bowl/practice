Although he is not Massenet, this composer wrote a Manon Lescaut. 17. Fine Arts/All (UIUC High School Solo 2008)
MODERATOR NOTE: Coyolxauhqui=[co-yol-ZOW-quee], Coatlicue [co-AHT-lee- cue] His name means “he of the south”, and he is often depicted holding a snake in one hand. His temple on the Great Pyramid was known as “Blue Heaven,” and those who died either in battle or during childbirth would serve in his palace. He slew many of this brothers and sisters after emerging fully armed from the womb of his mother Coatlicue, and he created the moon by cutting off the head of his sister Coyolxauhqui. Human sacrifices were necessary in his fight against the darkness, and it was he who told humans to look for an eagle on a cactus eating a serpent. FTP, identify this Aztec hummingbird deity, both the sun god and war god.
ANSWER: Huitzilopochtli [hoo-eetz-lee-POCHT-lee]

18. Fine Arts/All (PACE NSC 2011)
One of these works includes a descending chromatic scale in parallel thirds that sounds like laughter, leading it to be known as “The Devil’s Chuckle.” Three of these are marked Posato, or “sedate.” The ninth of these pieces alternates between imitations of a flute and a horn, representing a shepherdess and a hunter. The last of these pieces presents a twelve-bar theme and eleven variations, one of which includes left-hand pizzicato, before a finale rife with arpeggios. That piece, in A minor, was used by Rachmaninoff in his Rhapsody on a Theme of their composer. For 10 points, name these 24 monstrously difficult works for solo violin composed by an Italian virtuoso who was supposedly possessed by the Devil.
ANSWER: caprices by Niccolò Paganini [or capriccios by Niccolò Paganini]
