4. Fine Arts/All (NNT 2008)
With Imogen Cunningham, Willard van Dyke, and Edward Weston, this man founded the San Francisco-based Group f/64, and while working in New Mexico he was introduced to Alfred Stieglitz, who exhibited much of this man's work in his New York galleries. One of his most famous works depicts a small New Mexico town at sunset, Moonrise: Hernandez, but he is better known for photographs taken at the location of El Capitan and Half Dome.  Famous for his lifetime collaboration with the Sierra Club, For 10 points, name this photographer, most famous for his work in Yosemite National Park.
ANSWER: Ansel Adams

5. Fine Arts/Auditory (Harvard Fall Tournament 2012)
This composer added a coda to his piece "Beside a Spring" for his student Giovanni Sgambati. This man composed a series of works with slow "lassan" and fast "friska" portions transcribed for orchestra by Franz Doppler. Those include the "Carnival at Pest" and the "Rakoczi March." A famous set of etudes by him includes ones titled "Will o' the Wisp" and "Mazeppa." For 10 points, name this composer of the Mephisto Waltzes, Transcendental Etudes, and Hungarian Rhapsodies.

6. Fine Arts/All (Bulldog High School Academic Tournament (BHSAT) 2008)
A form of dance with 'English' and 'Finnish' variations, it was orchestrated into mainstream classical music largely thanks to the efforts of Astor Piazzolla. It is frequently danced either chest to chest or at arms length, and involves syncopated rhythmic footwork. Frequently thought of as an Argentinean dance, it was also developed in neighboring Uruguay. FTP, name this exciting and flamboyant dance form in 3, one of the most popular of ballroom dances

10. Fine Arts/All (OLEFIN 2011)
The opening of the Osanna of one of this man's pieces was taken from a cantata written for the coronation of Augustus III. That Osanna was part of a complete Catholic mass which this man wrote despite being Lutheran, Mass in B Minor. His Christmas Oratorio is composed on six cantatas intended to be played on separate days. His other oratorios are the Ascension and Easter ones. He also composed a book of preludes and fugues in all 24 major and minor keys, The Well- Tempered Clavier. For 10 points, name this German composer of the Brandenburg Concertos and the Goldberg Variations.
ANSWER: Johann Sebastian Bach [prompt on Bach]

Inspired by Virgil's Georgics, this work was later used in its composer's setting of his choral work Agnus Dei. This piece was premiered under conductor Arturo Toscanini in and was played during the announcements of the deaths of Franklin D. Roosevelt and John F. Kennedy. For 10 points, identify this work for string orchestra by the composer of Vanessa, Samuel Barber.
