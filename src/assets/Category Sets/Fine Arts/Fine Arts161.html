2. Fine Arts/All (PACE NSC 2010)
The gridded pink granite floor of this building's lobby imitates the five-by-three arrangement of its window bays. Its namesake plaza is the subject of William Whyte's The Social Life of Small Urban Spaces and is home to Alexander Calder's Ordinary. Its structure is mirrored by visible but non-structural I-beams, and it features tinted windows with blinds that can only be drawn in three positions as well as a bronze curtain wall. Located across from the Lever House on Park Avenue, it was to host a series of self-professed nauseating Rothko murals in its Four Seasons restaurant. For 10 points, name this International-style New York City skyscraper designed by Philip Johnson and Ludwig Mies van der Rohe.
ANSWER: the Seagram Building [prompt on 375 Park Avenue]

This composer's often performed fourth symphony's fourth movement is entitled Finale con epilogo fugato. 9. Fine Arts/All (BHSAT 2010)
A brief movement entitled "Purgatorio" appears in a work by this composer completed by Deryck Cooke, his tenth symphony. His fifth symphony begins with a funeral march in C-sharp minor and contains a famous Adagietto movement, A finch asks the singer, "Isn't it a fine world?" in the song "Ging heut Morgen übers Feld," which is quoted extensively in his first symphony. That song comes from the cycle Songs of a Wayfarer, and the symphony is nicknamed the "Titan." His second symphony, in C minor, uses texts by Klopstock, and his eighth symphony sets the Latin hymn "Veni Creator Spiritus" and is nicknamed for its large performing forces. For 10 points, identify this composer of the "Resurrection" Symphony and the Symphony of a Thousand.
ANSWER: Gustav Mahler
