1. Fine Arts/All (Prison Bowl 2010)
The subject's shoulder mirrors the peach he carries, in this artist's Boy with a Basket of Fruit. The title figure is surprised to touch Christ's wound in his Incredulity of St. Thomas. The two title figures both wear feathers in their hats, with one carrying a dagger, in The Cardsharps. A stone slab juts out toward the viewer in his Entombment of Christ. ANSWER: Michelangelo Merisi da Caravaggio [accept either]

9. Fine Arts/All (New Trier Scobol Solo 2011)
This artist trained Paolo Uccello (PAO-loh oo-CHEH-loh) and Donatello, and towards the end of his life he cast a bronze St. John the Baptist and Saint Stephen for the Orsanmichele (OR-sahn-mee-KAY-lay) in Florence. His most famous creations followed up earlier works by Andrea (ahn-DRAY-uh) Pisano, first placing eight heroes from the New Testament below twenty scenes from the New Testament, and he followed that up by working for twenty-seven years on ten panels showing scenes from the Old Testament. Before working on those pieces, he had to win a competition over Filippo Brunelleschi. Name this artist who created what are commonly called The Gates of Paradise, two sets of doors for the Florence Baptistery.
ANSWER: Lorenzo Ghiberti

10. Fine Arts/Visual (PACE NSC 2014)

A famous blue-paper drawing by Albrecht Dürer depicts two of them pressed together. An M.C. Escher lithograph depicts a piece of paper on which two of these things use pens to draw each other into existence.
