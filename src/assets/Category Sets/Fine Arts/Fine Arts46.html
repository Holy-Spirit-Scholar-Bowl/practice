After early representative work, including a painting of a boy in a striped shirt at Bennecourt and one of his first wife Camille Doncieux in a green dress, he used a new style in his depictions of the houses of Parliament. In addition to a series of haystacks, he also frequently painted the cathedral at Rouen, as well as a Japanese footbridge near his house in Giverny. For 10 points, name this painter of Impression, Sunrise, who was fond of water lilies.
ANSWER: Oscar-Claude Monet
