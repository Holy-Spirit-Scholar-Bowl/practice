1. Fine Arts/All (GDS Ben Cooper Memorial 2010)
This artist depicted a clerk, a farmer, and a soldier sleeping under a table-tree in The Land of Cockaigne. In another painting, a small crowd dances to the left of a cross while a magpie watches on the gallows. A skeleton army massacres humans in his Triumph of [*] Death. In one work in his series Labors of the Months, three figures return with dogs while skaters play on a frozen pond; the title figure makes a small splash in his Landscape with the Fall of Icarus. For 10 points, name this Dutch Renaissance painter, known for paintings such as The Peasant Wedding and The Hunters in the Snow.
ANSWER: Pieter Breughel the Elder [accept Peasant Breugel; Pieter Bruegel De Oudere]

2. Fine Arts/All (PACE NSC 2005)
Every day for a month, this painter went to the Pierre Loeb galley and added to one of his canvases, which by the end of the month portrayed a crust of bread, an apple with a fork in it, and a bottle of gin wrapped in newspaper. In 1967, he collaborated with José Llorens Artigas to create Wall of the Sun and Wall of the Moon, two murals for the Paris UNESCO office, which later commissioned him to design the Picasso Medal. For 10 points, name this surrealist painter of The Reaper and the afore-described Still Life With Old Shoe, which recorded his feelings upon leaving his Catalan homeland.
ANSWER: Joan Miró

Due in part to his ballet The Limpid Stream, this writer was harshly criticized in a newspaper article titled “Muddle Instead of Music”. Name this composer whose 13th symphony, based on the poetry of Yevgeny Yevtu- shenko, is nicknamed “Babi Yar”, and who also composed the opera Lady Macbeth of the Mtsensk District.

A selfportrait of a young Leonardo can be seen in an unfinished painting titled for these people commissioned for the San Donato a Scopeto. A scene named for these people is often set in a ruined house with a flimsy thatched roof, as in Botticelli's version. For 10 points, name these men commonly depicted bringing gifts to the infant Jesus.

11. Fine Arts/All (PACE NSC 2011)
