1. Religion/All (Fall Novice 2010)

4. Religion/None (PACE NSC 2015)

5. Religion/None (Harvard Fall Tournament 2012)
Yogacara posits "storehouse consciousness," consisting of seeds that ripen into a person's experience, to explain this concept. In Jainism, both vasudevas and prativasudevas acquire one variety of this while baladevas acquire comparatively less. Some traditions hold that different colors of this concept taint a person's soul. Bhakti, or devotion, and knowledge are alternative paths to this concept in the Bhagavad Gita, while arhats and bodhisattvas, like buddhas, are not affected by this. For 10 points, name this concept prevalent in Hinduism and Buddhism, the idea that actions committed in previous lifetimes influence one's prospects for rebirth.
ANSWER: karma [Accept kamma.]

7. Religion/All (NNT 2008)
