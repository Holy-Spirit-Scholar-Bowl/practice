3. Philosophy/All (Delta Burke 2003)
In it, one begins with the concept of God as that than which nothing greater can be conceived. To think of such a being as existing only in thought and not also in reality involves a contradiction, since a being that lacks real existence is not a being than which none greater can be conceived. A yet greater being would be one with the further attribute of existence. Thus the unsurpassably perfect being must exist; otherwise it would not be unsurpassably perfect. FTP, this is what argument for the existence of God first clearly formulated in the Proslogion by St. Anselm?
ANSWER: Ontological Argument
