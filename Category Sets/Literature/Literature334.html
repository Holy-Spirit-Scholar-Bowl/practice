1. Literature/All (Chitin 2008)
The first words of one title figure of this collection as a baby were "Drink, Drink, Drink." Because Brother John helps one against attacks by Picrochole, the father founds the Abbey of Thélème, which is a monastery where indulgence, marriage, and wealth are allowed. Later the other, with his friend Panurge, goes on an epic quest to find the Divine Bottle from which to drink to gain knowledge. For 10 points, name this collection about the son and grandson of Grandgousier, a medieval satire by Francois Rabelais.
ANSWER: The Life of Gargantua and Pantagruel [accept other words as part of the title, like Adventure, etc.]

2. Literature/All (MW GSAC XVII 2009)
A friend of the protagonist of this work attempts to convince him to go into the lumber business before the protagonist loses his job as a tutor after an affair is exposed because of the jealousy of a man in charge of a poorhouse, Valenod. Mathilde and Fouque later bury that protagonist after he is executed for the attempted murder of Madame de Renal. For 10 points, name this work about Julien Sorel, a novel by Stendhal.

13. Literature/All (PACE NSC 2005)
Cold descending squalls known as elvegusts are prevalent around them, as are sikussaks, which are chunks of old sea ice that become trapped in them. The deepest one on record is found in Skelton Inlet in Antarctica and the longest in the world is Greenland’s Scoresby Sund, though the only “true” one on the United States’ Atlantic coast is Somes Sound in Maine. They are generally formed in areas where rock is fairly resistant to erosion and where strong glaciation once took place, such as in New Zealand and Chiles. For 10 points, name these glaciated valleys that are filled in by seawater and are found in abundance in Norway.
ANSWER: fjord (fiord)
