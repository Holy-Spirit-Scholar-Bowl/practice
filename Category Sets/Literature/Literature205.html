1. Literature/All (HSAPQ Tournament 9 2009)
One of this man's poems describes the title entity as a "vulture, whose wings are dull realities" and a "true daughter of Old Time." Poems by this author of "Sonnet To Science" and "Ulalume" (OO-la-LOO-may) include one describing mimes eaten by the "crawling shape" of "The Conqueror Worm" and one set in a "kingdom by the sea." This poet of "Annabel Lee" describes the "tintinnabulation" of "The Bells" and opens another poem with "Once upon a midnight dreary, while I pondered, weak and weary," describing a creature fond of saying "nevermore."
