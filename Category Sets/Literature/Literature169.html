1. Literature/All (FKT 2013)
In one story by this author, an army officer wipes out two enemy divisions by accidentally starting a stampede of twenty-three hundred mules. In another story, a maker of medicinal ointments discovers the quality of his product improves when his son changes the main ingredient from stray dogs to unwanted children. In addition to “Jupiter Doke, Brigadier-General” and “Oil of Dog,” this writer described ‘peace’ as “a period of cheating between two periods of fighting” in a compilation titled The Devil’s Dictionary. For 10 points, name this author who wrote of Peyton Farquhar mistakenly thinking he escaped his own execution in “An Occurrence at Owl Creek Bridge.”
ANSWER: Ambrose Bierce

Alfred Prufrock” and The Waste Land. During World War II, his disillusionment with America, published in his Hugh Selwyn Mauberly: Life and Contacts, led to his support of Mussolini; after the war, a trial for treason in the U.S. would be cancelled after he was found legally insane.
