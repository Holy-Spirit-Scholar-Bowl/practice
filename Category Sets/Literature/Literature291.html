1. Literature/All (Harvard Fall Tournament 2010)
In one of this author’s novels, the title character shoots Glen Stanley, then takes poison to die alongside Lucy Tartan and his half-sister Isabel Banford. In another of this author’s works, a man is the colleague of Ginger Nut, Nippers, and Turkey, but after his previous career in a dead letter office, “prefers not” to do any work. This author collected that story into his The Piazza Tales. In addition to writing Pierre and “Bartleby the Scrivener,” this author created the crew of the Pequod, which includes Starbuck, Ishmael, and Captain Ahab. For 10 points, identify this author of Moby Dick.
ANSWER: Herman Melville

2. Literature/All (HSAPQ 4Q2 2009)

In another work by this author, the singing teacher David Gamut uses his slingshot and offers to take the place of "Le Cerf Agile." In a novel, this author described the marriage of Oliver Edwards to Elizabeth, the daughter of Judge Marmaduke Temple. Colonel Munro's daughter Cora is taken by Magua in a work by this author, which prompts Uncas's father Hawkeye to kill Magua. For 10 points, name this American author who wrote The Pioneers and The Last of the Mohicans.
ANSWER: James Fenimore Cooper

3. Literature/All (HSAPQ Tournament 16 2011)

Another of his poems wonders "Did he who made the lamb make thee?", and describes an animal "burning bright / in the forests of the night." For 10 points, name this poet whose Songs of Innocence and Experience includes "The Tyger."
ANSWER: William Blake
