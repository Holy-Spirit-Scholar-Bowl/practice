1. Literature/All (RAVE 2012)
This poet described a woman’s “polished eyes” in a work that begins by noting her “undulating, lustrous clothes.” This writer addresses one line to “Languorous Asia and burning Africa!” while saying that he shall plunge his head in the titular feature, “The Hair”. This man claimed “how sweet to go there and live together!” in “Invitation to a Journey”, and he described a bird that ship crews capture “for entertainment” in his poem “The Albatross”. This man’s most famous work begins with “To the Reader” and has sections named “Wine” and “Spleen and Ideal”. For 10 points, name this French poet who wrote The Flowers of Evil.
ANSWER: Charles Baudelaire

4. Literature/All (PACE NSC 2011)
