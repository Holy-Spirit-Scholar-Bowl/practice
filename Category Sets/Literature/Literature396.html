1. Literature/All (HSAPQ Tournament 8 2009)
One of this author's short stories tells of a girl who has an imaginary friend named Jimmy Jimoreeno. In another of his short stories, a boys' club leader named "The Chief" tells stories of Chinese bandit who was deformed when his head was placed in a vice grip. This author of "Uncle Wiggily in Connecticut" and "The Laughing Man" also wrote about a family of child prodigies including Seymour, who shoots himself after telling Sybil about bananafish. Another of his characters leaves Pencey Prep to visit his sister Phoebe, and loathes "phonies." For 10 points, name this author who created Holden Caulfield in The Catcher in the Rye
ANSWER: Jerome David Salinger

2. Literature/All (PACE NSC 2004)
He began his writing career as a journalist for The Californian, and he soon became known for short stories such as “M’Liss,” “Plain Language from Truthful James,” “Brown of Calaveras” and “The Idyl of Red Gulch.” All of those were published in the periodical that he edited, the Overland Monthly. For 10 points, name this American author of “The Outcasts of Poker Flat.”
ANSWER: Francis Bret Harte

3. Literature/All (BHSAT 2011)

Besides Rosmersholm and When We Dead Awaken, in another of his plays, the title character burns a manuscript by her former lover Lovborg, and is unhappily married to Professor Tesman. The protagonist of another of his plays leaves the title location after confessing her crime of forgery to her husband, Torvald. That character is Nora Helmer. For 10 points, identify this author of Hedda Gabbler and A Doll’s House, a Norwegian playwright?
ANSWER: Henrik Ibsen

4. Literature/All (HAVOC II 2009)

5. Literature/All (New Trier Scobol Solo 2011)
This composer wrote one symphonic poem about a girl who falls into a lake and gets into an argument with a water goblin, and another symphonic poem about a misbehaving boy who is killed by a noon witch. He also composed the symphonic poem The Wood Dove, and his eighth symphony has a flute bird call theme. This composer’s sixth symphony contains a furiant, and he also wrote eight humoresques, one of which became popular when it was attached to lyrics about flushing toilets. Name this composer whose first symphony is nicknamed The Bells of Zlonice (ZLOU-nees) and whose ninth symphony is subtitled From the New World.
ANSWER: Antonín Dvořák (d’-VAWR-zhak, but be generous)

6. Literature/All (SCOP Novice 2010)
In one work by this author, one nobody is excited about meeting another nobody, and in another poem by this poet, the speaker writes from after-death that, in between heaves, she was distracted by a "blue, uncertain, stumbling buzz." Another poem claimed "those who ne'er succeed" count "success the sweetest," while this poet also described "A carriage holding just ourselves/and immortality." Name this confessional poet of "I heard a fly buzz when I died," and "Because I could not stop for death," the "Belle of Amherst."
ANSWER: Emily Dickinson
