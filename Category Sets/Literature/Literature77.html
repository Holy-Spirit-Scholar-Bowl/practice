1. Literature/All (WUHSAC XI 2009)
The protagonist of this work tries to buy an egg from a knitting sheep, but ends up with Humpty Dumpty instead. Earlier in the day, she had wandered through a garden only to be criticized by an ill-tempered Tiger-Lily and then dragged a chess-board of criss-crossing streams by the Red Queen. The main character begins her adventures in a house where everything is backwards in, for ten points, what novel, a work by Lewis Carroll?
ANSWER: Through the Looking Glass: And What Alice Found There

2. Literature/All (HSAPQ ACF 3 2008)
G. Q. Heather-Greener studied them in migraine headache sufferers. One of them involved the formula for trimethylamin and a dose of propyl and was known as Irma's Injection. A biological view of them states they reflect cortical explanations of random neural signals, while a psychodynamic view distinguishes their "manifest content" and "latent content" and was studied by an Austrian who wrote "The Interpretation of" them. For 10 points, name these nighttime events that occur during REM sleep.
ANSWER: dreams

3. Literature/All (HSAPQ NSC 1 2008)
Hans Giebenrath befriends Hermann Heilner and drowns after sinking into mental illness in one of this author's works, and another of his works tells of the title character's prodding of Emil Sinclair to spiritual realization; those works are Beneath the Wheel and Demian. Mozart judges one of this author's characters after he meets Pablo and stabs Hermine in the Magic Theater. This author wrote about Joseph Knecht, who succeeds Thomas van der Trave as Magister Ludi. For 10 points, name this author of The Glass Bead Game, Der Steppenwolf, and Siddhartha.
ANSWER: Hermann Hesse

9. Literature/All (Weekend of Quizbowl Saturday Event 2009)
One part of this work contains the often excerpted "Forest Murmurs," and one character in that part exhanges three riddles with the Wanderer. That part opens as that character, Mime, is forging a sword. One of this work's two ensemble pieces comes after its namesakes mock an ugly character who tries to woo them; he then steals what they are guarding. That piece is the song of Woglinde, Wellgunde, and Flosshilde, the Rhinemaidens, and that ugly character is the dwarf Alberich, who has stolen the Rheingold.
