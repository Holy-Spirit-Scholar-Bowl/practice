1. Literature/All (Maggie Walker GSAC XVIII 2010)
In this work, a trip to Brighton is precipitated by Colonel Miller’s regiment relocating from Meryton, and after Christmas, one character stays with the Gardiners in London. Earlier, the protagonist is criticized for getting mud on her clothes while walking three miles to visit her sick sister. The protagonist disapproves of the marriage between the officer Wickham and her younger sister Lydia, and after being confronted by Catherine Bingley, the protagonist becomes engaged to Mr. Darcy. For 10 points, name this novel about Jane and Elizabeth Bennet written by Jane Austen.
ANSWER: Pride and Prejudice

2. Literature/All (Weekend of Quizbowl Saturday Event 2009)
Weekend of Quizbowl Saturday Tournament 2009 Round 3 RTB (2/2 Hist, 2/2 Sci, 1/1 Lit, 1/1 RMP, 1/1 SS, 1/1 Arts) In one of this author’s works, John Goldencalf meets four talking monkeys, while his first novel ended with the marriage of Emily Moseley to the Earl of Pendennyss. His “Literary Offenses” were enumerated in a Mark Twain essay that criticized the unrealism of some of his characters, who include Chingachgook and a man called “Pathfinder”. For 10 points, identify this author whose novels about Natty Bumppo were collected in his Leatherstocking Tales, which also included his The Last of the Mohicans.
