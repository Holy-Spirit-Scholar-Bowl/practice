1. Literature/All (PACE NSC 2005)
He lives in a mansion in Fauborg St. Germain with a rich young man he met in a bookstore. He takes the line “So funereal a design, if it is not worthy of Atreus, it is worthy of Thyestes” to describe the scheme of Minister D to hide a valuable object in plain sight, and he exonerates a French sailor named Le Bon from crimes committed by his pet, all to prove his superior intellect. For 10 points, identify this detective who solved “The Mystery of Marie Roget,” “The Purloined Letter” and “The Murders in the Rue Morgue.”
ANSWER: C. Auguste Dupin
