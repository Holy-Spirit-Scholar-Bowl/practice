1. Literature/All (PACE NSC 2004)
His bleak poem “1967,” written “five-score summers” earlier, ends with the hope “that thy worm will be my worm, Love.” His other poems such as “Channel Firing” and “The Darkling Thrush” remain popular, but he is likely better known for novels such as The Dynasts. For 10 points, name this English author who wrote of Wessex in The Mayor of Casterbridge, Tess of the D’urbervilles, and Far From the Madding Crowd.
ANSWER: Thomas Hardy
