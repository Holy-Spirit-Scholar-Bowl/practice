1. Social Science/All (HSAPQ Tournament 15 2011)
This phenomenon’s effect on interest rates is named for Mundell and Tobin. The rate of this phenomenon is given by Gordon’s Triangle, and its effects are modeled by the “Shoe Leather Cost.” The rate at which it occurs is calculated by dividing the annual percentage change in the Consumer Price Index by time.
