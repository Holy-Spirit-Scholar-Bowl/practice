4. Fine Arts/All (QuAC I 2008)
Before A Southern Convent is one of his few works for chorus, and his String Quartet was said to have influenced Debussy. 7. Fine Arts/All (WUHSAC XI 2009)
One of his last published pieces was the seven-song collection Klopstocks Oder und Lieder, and among his first were the six trio sonatas he published in London. He would become known more for operas, such as his debut, Artaserse, and his pre-Mozart version of P. Metastasio's La Clemenza di Tito. In his mature operas with Raniero Calzabigi, including Paride ed Elena, he threw out unaccompanied, florid recitative in favor of “simplicity, truth, and naturalness,” as stated in his foreword to Alceste. ANSWER: Christoph Willibald Gluck
