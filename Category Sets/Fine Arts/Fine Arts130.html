1. Fine Arts/Visual (Maggie Walker GSAC 2012)
In this work, a small mug can be seen next to a fisherman on the bottom right, and a small rock structure with an entrance can be seen on the right side of a white cliff formation. A man with a staff stares at the sky with a dog next to him, and around that man is a group of white sheep. A ship in full sail can be seen above the title figure in this work, in which a farmer plows the field with his horse at the highest elevation of the painting. Nobody in the painting notices the two legs of the title figure, which can be seen flailing in ocean. For 10 points, name this painting largely attributed to Pieter Bruegel the Elder.
ANSWER: Landscape with the Fall of Icarus

2. Fine Arts/All (New Trier Scobol Solo 2011)
One member of this art movement, Charles Camoin, painted a portrait of another member, Albert Marquet (al-bair mar-kay), in addition to painting one of the two founders of this movement. That leader painted boats seen through an open window, Landscape at Collioure (coal-your), and Woman with a Hat. The other leader of this movement, who painted Charing Cross Bridge while on a trip to London funded by Ambroise Vollard, was André Derain. The first and more famous leader of this movement, who painted his wife with a green stripe down the middle of her face, was Henri Matisse (ahn-ree mah-tees). Name this early twentieth century movement known for using bright colors whose name is French for “wild beasts”.
ANSWER: Les Fauves [or Fauvism; accept word forms; prompt on wild beasts]

3. Fine Arts/All (Centennial (MD) Housewrite 2011)
His first major commissioned work was modeled by a Belgian soldier, and portrays a nude male with his arms raised in the air entitled “The Age of Bronze.”. In 1880 he was commissioned to create a portal for a museum in Paris, inspired by Dante’s _The Inferno_. Many of his other works were derived from small reliefs in the gates, such as a man, envisioned as Dante himself, sitting in deep thought. For 10 points, name this French sculptor who created works such as The Gates of Hell, The Kiss, and The Thinker.
ANSWER: Auguste Rodin
