18. Fine Arts/All (MOHIT (Thomas Jefferson) 2009)
This man was a dealer in Japanese art as well as an architect, and he created the Usonian house. By 1901, he had completed about 50 design projects, including many houses in his home town of Oak Park. Many of his residential designs were called Prairie Houses due to their low open floor plans, designed to feature unfinished materials. A project from his later period was a residence composed of cantilevered limestone and concrete balconies in Bear Run, Pennsylvania, which was one of the 400 buildings that he completed in his lifetime. FTP, name this Illinois leader of the Prairie School movement of architecture, the designer of Falling Water and the Guggenheim Museum in New York.
ANSWER: Frank Lloyd Wright
