3. Fine Arts/All (NTSS 2010)
This building was originally named Hurtig and Seamon's New Burlesque Theatre, and its theme song was “I May Be Wrong but I Think You're Wonderful”. Ralph Cooper hosted a popular show from this location on Wednesday nights that broke ground when it featured Benny Carter and Sixteen Gorgeous Hot Steppers. It became known as the place “Where Stars are Born and Legends are Made”, and its famous attractions were its Amateur Hour and its Show- time. Some of the most successful James Brown albums were recorded at this theater, and it is where his memorial ser- vice was held. This theater also played a prominent role in launching the careers of Bessie Smith, Billie Holiday, Stevie Wonder, and The Jackson 5. Name this performance venue located in Harlem.
ANSWER: The Apollo Theater

4. Fine Arts/Auditory (PACE NSC 2016)
This composer called for a solo violin with its strings tuned up a whole step in his Symphony No. 4, which includes a slow third movement marked "Ruhevoll" (roo-heh- "fole"). An offstage posthorn plays a solo in his Symphony No. 3, whose opening theme is introduced by eight horns. His second symphony includes an alto setting of his song "Urlicht." A lone trumpet plays a fanfare in C-sharp minor to begin the funeral march first movement of his fifth symphony. His first symphony includes a funeral march rendition of "Frère Jacques," while his aforementioned fifth symphony includes an Adagietto fourth movement sometimes considered a declaration of love to his wife Alma. For 10 points, name this Austrian composer of symphonies nicknamed Resurrection and of a Thousand.
ANSWER: Gustav Mahler

10. Fine Arts/All (PACE NSC 2005)
Among the building projects undertaken by this monarch were two enormous obelisks, then the largest in the world and still standing in the Red Chamber at Karnak. Ruling under the regnal name Maatkare and clearly attempting to emulate the earlier Sobekneferu, this monarch also sent an expedition to Punt. While archaeologists have recently begun to believe that the erasing of names long blamed on the next ruler actually came much later, someone was clearly offended by such actions as this ruler’s assumption of the title “Pharoah” and the wearing of the ceremonial false beard. For 10 points name this successor of Thutmose I, the most powerful woman to rule Egypt before Cleopatra.
ANSWER: Hatshepsut [accept Maatkare before it is read]
