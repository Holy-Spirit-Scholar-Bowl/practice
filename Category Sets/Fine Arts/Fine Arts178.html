16. Fine Arts/All (PACE NSC 2005)
This term can be used for work as diverse as that of Otto Wagner and Joseph Olbrich, who opposed Wagner’s classicism and formed the Vienna Secession. It is exemplified by Victor Horta’s Hotel Tassel, inspired by the works of Charles Rennie Macintosh. Architect Hector Guimard used it for the Paris Metro signs, and it also popped up as Spain’s “modernisme” and Germany’s Jugendstil (YOO gind shteel). For 10 points, name this movement also practiced by Antonio Gaudi and shown at the Universal Exposition of 1900, exemplified by the painting of Gustav Klimt and the glassmaking of Louis Tiffany.
ANSWER: Art Nouveau [accept Jugendstil before it is read; prompt on modernisme before it is read]

17. Fine Arts/All (HFT 2008)
Its architect designed a clock with an hour hand only and a weathervane which can be read from indoors, both located in its east portico. It was one of the earliest buildings with a dumbwaiter, and its dining room table was folded up between meals. Several of its rooms are named for their shape, such as the “North Octagonal” and “South Square” rooms. While the master bedroom incorporates elements from the Roman temple of Fortuna Virilis, the most major influence on it was the villas of Andrea Palladio that its inhabitant and designer had seen while abroad; the Villa Rotonda, for example, inspired its famous dome. FTP, name this Virginia home of Thomas Jefferson.
ANSWER: Monticello
