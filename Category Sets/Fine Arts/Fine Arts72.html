1. Fine Arts/Visual (PACE NSC 2012)
He's not Raphael, but he created a bust of a bearded Bindo Altoviti. An effete nude touches his left hand to his curly hair as he looks down and to his right in this artist's Narcissus. After fleeing Italy in the Sack of Rome, he was commissioned to make The Nymph of Fontainebleau. One of his works sees a man seated on a sea shell drawn by four horses. A nude with wings coming out from his feet and head holds a sword and the head of his foe in a work by this man now in Florence's Loggia dei Lanzi. His best known work, sculpted in gold, shows Ceres grabbing her left breast while a trident is held by a mannerist Neptune. For 10 points, name this artist of Perseus Beheading Medusa and The Salt Cellar of Francis I.
